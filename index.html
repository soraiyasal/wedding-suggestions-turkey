Istanbul

1. Historical Peninsula
+
Must See
  • Hagia Sophia
  • Blue Mosque
  • Basilica Cistern
  • Suleymaniye Mosque (a bit out of the way, but worth it)
Worth Your Time
  • Topkapı Palace / Museum (if you have at least a few hours)
  • Grand Bazaar & Egyptian Bazaar
  • Hamams (lots in the area if interested)
Eats Nearby
  • Balık Ekmek: Fish sandwich streetfood in Eminönü
  • Olden 1772: Cool restaurant in the area
  • Pandeli Restaurant: Traditional Turkish food
2. Taksim, Beyoğlu, Karaköy
+
Highlights
  • İstiklal Street: Walking from Taksim Square to Beyoğlu—shops, churches, passageways
  • Galata Tower: Skip going up; restaurants nearby have the same view
  • Art Museums: Istanbul Modern, Pera Museum, SALT Galata
Restaurants
  • Biz İstanbul: Amazing view, modern take on traditional Turkish cuisine
  • Okra İstanbul: Amazing view, fine dining
  • Mürver Restaurant
  • Mükellef Karaköy
  • GalataPort: Port/mall with restaurants by Bosphorus
3. Bosphorus Coast & Rumeli Fortress
+
The Experience

All the way from Tarabya to Karaköy, by the water, is great. On a good day, you can walk any part of it.

Recommended Spots
  • Arnavutköy: Good for seafood and nice views; close to Rumeli Fortress
  • Bebek to Tarabya: Very peaceful, amazing views and nice walks (for those with more time)
4. Kadıköy-Moda
+
The Vibe

Hip, young neighbourhood with lots of shops, coffee shops, music venues on the Anatolian side.

Pro Tip

Take the ferry from Beşiktaş or Karaköy to Kadıköy and back. It's cheap and totally worth it—amazing view and experience.

5. Beşiktaş: Dolmabahçe Palace
+
What's There

Lots of shops and historical places like Dolmabahçe Palace.

Note: Beşiktaş is more of a local commute square, so it can get chaotic at times.

What to Eat
+
Turkish Kebap

Many different types—a bit different than "kebab" in Europe. The classic: Adana Kebap (spicy) or Urfa Kebap (not spicy). In a good restaurant, get a "plate." In casual spots, try the dürüm (wrapped) version.

Döner

Slightly different than döner in Europe. In a good restaurant, try a plate or Iskender (döner plate with special tomato sauce and butter). In casual spots, get a wrap or sandwich.

Turkish Delight

Ali Muhiddin Hacıbekir (Best traditional Turkish delight, family-owned since 1777). Locations in Eminönü, Taksim, Kadıköy. Don't buy anywhere else, especially Grand Bazaar or İstiklal Street.

More to Try
  • Lahmacun & Pide: Turkish meat-pie and flatbread
  • Seafood: Go to a nice restaurant by the sea, try whatever fish is in season with small mezes & calamari
  • Desserts: Baklava (try at Güllüoğlu—pistachio is classic), Künefe, Sütlaç
For Foodies

Street Food: Börek, Kokoreç, Islak hamburger, Balık ekmek, Midye dolma, Kumpir

Regional Kebaps: Cağ Kebabı, Testi Kebabı, Taş Kebabı

Home Cooking: Mantı, Sarma, İçli Köfte, Karnıyarık, Hünkar Beğendi, Dolma, Kuru fasulye

Bodrum

The essentials

Bodrum Castle + marina walk. Old Town for an easy evening stroll.

The best Bodrum day

Boat trip. Swim in hidden coves. Repeat.

Sunset spots

Gümüşlük (laid-back harbour) or Yalıkavak (polished marina + dinner).

Chill beach day

Bitez. Relaxed, easy, exactly what you need.

A little myth for you

Bodrum was once Halicarnassus—home to a spring called Salmakis. According to Ovid, the water nymph Salmacis fell for Hermaphroditus and clung to him in the water, praying they'd never be separated. The gods listened. They became one being. Ancient writers said anyone who drank from the spring would be "softened" by its magic. We like to think it just makes you fall a little more in love with the place.
